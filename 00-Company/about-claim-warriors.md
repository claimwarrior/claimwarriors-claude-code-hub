# About Claim Warriors

## What We Do

Claim Warriors is a public adjusting firm that represents homeowners and property owners in insurance claims. When a customer has property damage (water, fire, roof, etc.) and their insurance company underpays, denies, or undervalues their claim, we step in to fight for a fair settlement.

We are NOT the insurance company. We work on behalf of the policyholder against the carrier.

## How We Make Money

We take claims on a contingency basis. The customer signs a contract, and we get paid a percentage of the additional recovery we secure from the insurance company.

The business lives and dies on two things:
1. **Volume** -- how many viable claims we bring in
2. **Claim quality** -- filtering out garbage claims early so the back office doesn't waste hours on claims worth nothing

## Business Scale

- 788+ completed (signed) contracts in the system
- ~796 customers who were contacted but didn't sign (future outreach opportunity)
- Multiple claim types: water damage, roof damage, fire damage, denied claims, underpaid claims, new claims, miscellaneous
- Active sales and back office teams

## Two Departments

### Front Office (Customer-Facing)
- **Sales**: Inbound and outbound calls. Reps pre-qualify leads, collect claim info, send contracts during or after the call
- **Customer Support**: Handle ongoing customer questions, status updates, escalations

See [[front-office]] for detailed process.

### Back Office (Operations)
- **Claims Processing**: Adjusters review damage, write estimates, negotiate with carriers
- **Consultants**: Advise on claim routing -- inspection, attorney referral, direct negotiation
- **Administration**: File management, documentation, compliance

See [[back-office]] for detailed process.

## Core Business Problem AI Solves

Jo's team spends significant time (at ~$50/hr) working claims that turn out to be garbage. Example: a customer says their claim is worth $500k, the back office works 15 hours on it, only to discover the claim is worthless. AI needs to catch these early -- during the sales call or immediately after -- by analyzing what the customer says, the pictures they provide, and the data available.
