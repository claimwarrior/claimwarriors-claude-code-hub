# Objection/Response Pairs

Ranked by frequency across 2,800+ successful sales calls. Each objection includes the most effective response strategy with real quotes from CW reps.

---

## 1. Complexity / Process Confusion

**Frequency**: #1 across all batches (16-29 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 85-92%

**What the customer says**:
- "I have a million questions."
- "So I signed a contract with you — does that mean you interact with insurance on my behalf? I'm out of the loop?"
- "I don't know what to do at this point."
- "I'm just overwhelmed about this whole thing."
- "How does appraisal work? Could you explain that to me properly?"

**Best response strategy**: Education (resolves 70-80% of the time)

**How to respond**:
Break the complex down into simple. Don't use insurance jargon. Explain step by step what happens next and what the customer does vs. what CW does.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "No, very simple. You don't have to talk to anyone anymore. All you need to do is give me the policy number, the claim number, your full address, your email address, and that's it. We'll send the document."

> "So the state empowers what is called a public adjuster to be on the insured side. We basically adjust the claim so that it's favorable to you — that's really why you entered into the contract with your insurance company."

> "Not necessarily — there are 2 different departments involved with insurance claims. So a lot of times, even if we get involved, the ALE side just continues."

> "So in order for you to qualify for appraisal, they have to have opened up coverage. They gave money, but they didn't give enough money."

## 2. Trust / Credibility / Skepticism

**Frequency**: #2 across all batches (10-22 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 80-87%

**What the customer says**:
- "How come you guys aren't listed under the Better Business Bureau?"
- "I already had a private adjuster who dogged around and acted like he didn't know me."
- "I don't want to sign something without looking at your ratings."
- "But y'all guaranteed to win this case though, right?"
- "We just don't wanna be scammed."
- "Are y'all actually representing me? They're saying you haven't contacted them."

**Best response strategy**: Empathy + Direct Answer (combined approach)

**How to respond**:
Don't get defensive. Validate the concern. Then provide concrete proof — license number, Google reviews, the "no win no fee" guarantee. Honesty about what you *can't* guarantee is more convincing than promises.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "We're gonna try. If I don't think we can win, I'll let you know. I won't string you along. But I just wanna review — I need to look at your policy, I need to look at all the documents."

> "Worst case scenario, can't win you anything, then we don't charge you anything. And that's exactly what the document says."

> "This is our license number. That's our license to do public adjusting work in the state."

> "It's a surprise to you, but it's not a surprise to me. [laughs] That's the psychological games they play with you. They say 'we're closing the claim,' but that actually doesn't mean anything."

> "Exactly. That's the reason why you hired us so that we can do the talking for you. We'll take care of you."

## 3. Cost / Fees

**Frequency**: #3 across all batches (11-19 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 85-100%

**What the customer says**:
- "So what's your typical percentage?"
- "25 percent seems a little high."
- "Is there any way to go lower than that?"
- "I don't wanna pay 20 percent to you guys and 20 percent to them."
- "So you're saying it's not gonna be out of my pocket no matter what?"
- "Do I get nothing? You guys get everything and I get nothing?"

**Best response strategy**: Direct Answer + Reframe

**How to respond**:
State the percentage clearly. Immediately reframe: the fee only applies to *additional* money won. Emphasize "no win, no fee." Then shift to the value — "We usually get 2-3x what contractors quote, so even after our fee, you come out way ahead."

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "We only charge 20 percent to the addition. The contract says we only charge 20 percent for what we win you from today. It doesn't count what came before."

> "That's a great question. You don't have to pay us anything. We only charge a percentage. And if we couldn't get you anything, we don't charge."

> "Well, we work off a percentage. That way we're motivated to make sure we get you the highest amount."

> "The 30 percent will be applied to the amount after the deductible, but we're gonna go for something way higher. Our estimates are usually 2x or 3x higher than what contractors quote."

> "We would charge 25 percent. But since your claim is larger, we'll settle for 20 percent."

## 4. Time / Delays

**Frequency**: #4 across all batches (7-15 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 75-85%

**What the customer says**:
- "How long does this take?"
- "It's already been so many months."
- "I'm just getting very frustrated with the whole situation."
- "I don't have time to sit here and do all that."
- "It just feels like it's getting really slow again."

**Best response strategy**: Direct Answer + Empathy (+ Urgency when needed)

**How to respond**:
Give a specific timeline ("3-4 months"). Acknowledge the frustration. Then explain why acting *now* is better than waiting — the insurance company benefits from delay, not the customer.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "It'll take 3 to 4 months. The first thing we'll do is wait for all the documents from the insurance company."

> "The only thing is your claim is still ongoing. The insurance company — they're experts at getting people to just wait. What they're gonna try to do is drag your claim really long, and sometimes people reach out and it's too late already."

> "But, Tyler, I promise you by end of March, I'll resolve everything for you. I'm still within that time frame."

> "I understand. But I'm gonna set a reminder for myself. I'll give them 2 days. I'm gonna follow up 2 days later to see what they're doing."

## 5. Prior Bad Experience

**Frequency**: #5 across all batches (6-15 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 80-87%

**What the customer says**:
- "I already had an adjuster before and he screwed it up."
- "I've been burned. We've been burnt as a family."
- "I had a couple attorneys that said they'd help, and nobody ever called back."
- "The other guy will do the same thing."
- "I was literally thinking I just got scammed."

**Best response strategy**: Empathy + Education + Reframe

**How to respond**:
Validate the bad experience fully — don't dismiss it. Then differentiate CW. Explain specifically *how* CW's approach is different (licensing, process, accountability). Make a personal commitment.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "I apologize, and I hate that you've had a bad experience thus far. But I can assure you, I'll handle your case personally, and I assure you you won't have any issues."

> "You sound like a nice guy so they take advantage of you. The thing is they're not nice, so you can't play nicer than them. You have to go at them aggressively. And we know how to do that."

> "You called the right place. We specialize in fire claims, and you're right — insurance companies will pay as little as possible until a professional with a license is involved."

> "And if you ever did wanna back out, we're totally okay with that. But why don't you just let us show you what we can do for you in the next couple of weeks?"

## 6. Missing Documentation

**Frequency**: #6 across all batches (3-11 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 85-90%

**What the customer says**:
- "I don't have those receipts — they were destroyed in the fire."
- "The credit card I used, I no longer use."
- "I don't even have the list. Can you send me the list?"
- "I'm 71 and computer illiterate."

**Best response strategy**: Direct Answer + Problem-Solving

**How to respond**:
Never make the customer feel like missing docs kill the claim. Offer alternatives: photos, bank statements, memory-based lists. Offer to handle it for them. Take the burden.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "Sometimes banks do keep credit card statements for up to 7 years."

> "If it's possible, you could find a picture of that toolbox somewhere in your photos just to prove it's not a Harbor Freight toolbox. That's what we'll need."

> "Send it to me. I want you to send everything to me, and we will send what needs to be sent to them."

> "I can text you a link — it's gonna be like an online Excel. You can pop it up on the computer. And I'll give you an example."

## 7. Policy Issues

**Frequency**: #7 across all batches (3-8 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 80-86%

**What the customer says**:
- "Insurance company says our policy doesn't cover that."
- "They denied it citing 'mold' and 'slow seepage.'"
- "I have a cash value policy, not replacement cost."
- "They said it was wear and tear, not storm damage."

**Best response strategy**: Education + Direct Answer

**How to respond**:
Request the policy for review. Explain that insurance companies misinterpret policies in their favor — and CW knows how to read policies the way the state intended. Specific strategies depend on the claim type.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "Alright, if they haven't sent the denial letter yet, the sooner we get involved the better — because we might be able to prevent them from denying you simply by submitting our license on your behalf. Sometimes it scares the insurance company."

> "Your policy has an exclusion for business. But some policies let you get an endorsement to add limited business coverage. Let me review the actual policy language."

> "There's a lot of buyouts and collaborations, but what you need to pay attention to is which insurance company actually issued your policy."

## 8. Already Have a Contractor / Prior Adjuster

**Frequency**: #8 across all batches (3-7 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 75-85%

**What the customer says**:
- "My contractor is already fighting them."
- "I already had an adjuster — a private adjuster — and he screwed it up."
- "My contractor says you guys wouldn't be entitled to dwelling money."
- "I already negotiated part of it on my own."

**Best response strategy**: Education + Reframe

**How to respond**:
Clarify that CW's fee only applies to *additional* money won after today. Differentiate CW's role from a contractor's role. If there's a prior adjuster, explain the process for switching.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "It only counts for the additional money we get you from today. The money that was committed to you prior to our agreement — we don't touch that."

> "It's better to get your own independent contractor, someone who's not connected to the insurance company, because you just can't trust them if they're connected."

> "So the money you use to pay your contractor comes from the insurance proceeds. But the money the insurance pays — that's what we negotiate to be higher."

## 9. Low Value / Not Worth It

**Frequency**: #9 across all batches (2-5 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 70-80%

**What the customer says**:
- "25 percent is a lot, and I don't wanna end up getting less than I would have gotten anyway."
- "I don't really have anything that valuable."
- "The estimate says it's not enough damage to interest you guys."

**Best response strategy**: Reframe + Value Amplification

**How to respond**:
Challenge the customer's assumption about claim value. Insurance companies consistently underpay — CW typically gets 2-3x contractor quotes. If the claim genuinely is too small (<$20k), be honest: "This claim is too small for our firm" — don't waste their time or yours.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "Well, so far, we've gotten 0 percent of nothing either." [when customer worried about fees on a low amount]

> "If they end up paying $5,000 on our assessment and damages are $50,000 — that's what we fight for."

> "We usually push up the value of that estimate by 2x or 3x."

## 10. Old Claim

**Frequency**: #10 across all batches (2-5 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 70-80%

**What the customer says**:
- "This happened over a year ago."
- "The claim has been sitting there forever."
- "Is it too late?"

**Best response strategy**: Urgency + Education

**How to respond**:
Check statute of limitations (varies by state — typically 2 years for property). If within window, use urgency: "We need to get started right away." If borderline, be transparent about the challenge but emphasize that CW can still fight.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "Your claim is kinda old. So we need to get started right away because we have until November to do this — it takes 2 years to negotiate."

> "Even though your policy is expiring, you're still covered. It doesn't matter. They still gonna pay. My main goal is that you already opened the claim."

## 11. Repairs Already Done

**Frequency**: #11 across all batches (2-4 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 75-85%

**What the customer says**:
- "I already fixed the roof."
- "We already started the repairs."
- "The mold remediation company already came."

**Best response strategy**: Problem-Solving + Reassurance

**How to respond**:
Repairs don't kill the claim. CW can work from existing photos, the customer's documentation, and the insurance company's own inspection records. Focus on recovering underpaid amounts.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "As long as you got pictures, that's fine. Just take pictures. We'll argue from the documentation, and we'll still go on site even if it's been repaired already."

> "We can work off your pictures and the insurance company's pictures, and we might still come over for an inspection. We'll focus on the policy limits."

## 12. "I Need to Talk to My Spouse / Attorney / Someone Else"

**Frequency**: #12 across all batches (2-4 mentions per 100 calls)
**Resolution rate**: 60-70% (lowest resolution rate)

**What the customer says**:
- "I need to talk to my husband about it."
- "Let me check with my attorney first."
- "I need to think about it."

**Best response strategy**: Soft Urgency + Offer to Include

**How to respond**:
Don't pressure. Offer to get on a three-way call with the decision maker. Provide clear information they can share. Set a specific follow-up time so the conversation doesn't die.

**Real rep quotes that worked**:
> "If you wanted to jump on a call with your attorney, I have no issue. Your attorney knows how to read estimates and he will understand what I'm talking about."

> "Perfect. I'm gonna send you this info. If you do decide to go with us, I can get on a call and do a 3-way conversation."
